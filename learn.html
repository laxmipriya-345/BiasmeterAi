What is AI Bias?

AI bias occurs when machine learning systems make unfair decisions that disadvantage certain groups of people.

It's not about the AI being "racist" or "sexist" intentionally, but about learning patterns from biased historical data.

Which scenario demonstrates AI bias?
Loan approvals: 80% men vs 45% women with same credit scores
Weather app predicting 75°F temperature
Calculator showing 2+2=4 for everyone

How Bias Creeps In

Bias enters AI systems through:

  • Biased training data (historical discrimination)
  • Flawed algorithm design (ignoring protected attributes)
  • Feedback loops (biased outcomes reinforcing bias)
  • Lack of diversity in development teams
Quick fact check: True or False?
AI can inherit bias from human decisions in training data
AI is completely objective and cannot be biased

Real-World Case Study: Amazon Hiring AI

Problem: In 2018, Amazon discovered their AI hiring tool was penalizing resumes with words like "women's" (as in "women's chess club") and graduates from all-women's colleges.

Root Cause: The AI was trained on 10 years of hiring data from male-dominated tech industry, learning to prefer male candidates.

Solution: Regular bias audits + diverse training data + human oversight.

How to Detect & Fix AI Bias

🛡️ Prevention

  • Diverse training data
  • Fairness constraints
  • Regular bias testing

🔍 Detection

  • Statistical parity tests
  • Disparate impact analysis
  • Real-time monitoring
